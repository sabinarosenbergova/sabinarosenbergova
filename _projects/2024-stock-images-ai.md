Together with Alberto Romele and Dario Rodighiero, I examined how stock images depict artificial intelligence — specifically the recurring visual motif of a human face or body fragmenting into particles, pixels, or voxels. Drawing on a qualitative and quantitative analysis of around 7,500 images from Shutterstock, we argue that these images are symptoms of a datafied worldview. The article combines Panofsky's iconology with Didi-Huberman's symptomatic reading to unpack what these ubiquitous images quietly tell us about how we imagine AI — including an unexpected conclusion about the dominance of blue.
