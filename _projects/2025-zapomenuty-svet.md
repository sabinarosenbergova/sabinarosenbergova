This short article, written for a broader intellectual audience as part of a journal issue on *Women and Work*, explores the fate of former female convents dissolved in the 18th and 19th centuries. It reflects on the artworks produced within these spaces and their transformation into museum settings—often stripped of their original context. The article highlights how these institutions tend to overlook the histories of the women who lived, created, cared for, and animated these objects, which are now frequently exhibited as supposedly gender-neutral artifacts in museums and galleries.

*The article reflects my research funded by the MSCA Postdoctoral Fellowship "Exploring the Transformation of Female Religious Spaces and Objects into Modern Museum Contexts."*
